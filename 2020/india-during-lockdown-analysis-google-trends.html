India During Lockdown : A Perspective From Analysing Google Trends Data

India had one of the stringest lockdown during the pandemic. The entire nation had to spend their time inside their houses. In this blog, I try to make an analysis of Indians life during the lockdown. Google Trends are a great tools as it tells us what people are searching for in real time. This can be used to measure interests in a particular topic, in a particular region and at a particular time. I use google trends to analyse Indian's interests/activities during the past 12 months (19 Dec 2019 - 19 Dec 2020).

The above graph shows the interest over time of the search term "lockdown". We see four spikes in the graph - the four spikes corresponds to the four phases of lockdown imposed by the Indian government starting from 25 March 2020 to 31 May 2020. During the lockdown, more people are bored as the search for the term "boredom" has spiked up during last week of March.

The above chart gives us a considerable evidence of Indians being bored during lockdown. With malls, shopping centers, cinema halls closed, we see that Indians are bored and this brings us to our next question - How did Indians spent time during the lockdown? The analysis following is based on the assumption that higher search interest correlates with higher takers for the activity.

Many people use their free time to learn new skills. The search interest for the term "learn" reaches a peak during mid-April.

To find the skills learnt by people, we can make use of the related queries section in google trends. For the analysis on what people learnt during the period, I look on the related queries section of the queries "learn" and "how to learn". People have acquired skills on coding, learnt spoken languages and dance. The popular spoken languages learnt by people are english, hindi, tamil and telugu. Python was the mose popular coding language during this period.

During the lockdown, there was a rise in the use of OTT platforms like Amazon prime and Netflix. More people started consumed content from OTT platforms.

Amazon Prime was more popular than Netflix in the three southern states - Tamil Nadu, Telangana and Andhra Pradesh. In the rest of India, Netflix was more popular than Amazon Prime. The popular Netflix shows among Indians were Jamtara, Extraction, Mismatched, Freud, A Suitbale Boy, Never Have I Ever and Money Heist. Similarly, the popular shows in Amazon Prime were Soorarai Pottru, Panchayat, Paatal Lok, Gulabo Sitabo, Penguin and Breathe: Into the Shadows. These were found by looking at the related queries section in google trends for the queries "Netflix" and "Amazon Prime".

People have also tried Do it yourself tasks at home. There was a considerable interest on making crafts, face mask, gifts. People have tried making hand santiziers. There was a considerable increase in interest on Cooking and Baking among the people. This is illustrated by a rise in search interest of the queries "cooking" and "baking" which reached a peak near mid-April.

Looking at the realted queries section for cooking, it shows Paneer Butter Masala and Chicken as the food items which more number of people tried out during the lockdown period. Surprisingly, Chinese food and country-life blogger Li Ziqi have also been popular in India. To dig a bit deeper, I searcherd for the query "how to make" and choosed category Food and Drink in google trends. Next, I looked at the related queries section and found the popular food items tried by people. They are dalgona coffee, cake, cream, chocolate, samosa, aam pappad, pasta, paneer, pizza and pani puri. A bit on the negative side here: people have also searched for how to make alcohol and whiskey at home. Jharkand had the highest number of searched on how to make alocohol followed by West Bengal, Kerala and Tamil Nadu.

The search interest for the term Cooking peaked during mid-April, and a bit unsurpisingly, the search interest for "Weight Loss" peaked at early June.

People have primarily taken diet methods and physical fitness exercise for weight loss. In diet, Keto diet and Japanese water therapy found increased takers for weight loss during the lockdown. Apart from these, people have taken cycling, yoga, surya namaskar and exercise to reduce weight. Interest for yoga increased approximately by 27% between March and April and it reached a peak during June 21 (International Yoga Day).

There was an increase in insomnia experienced by people during lockdown. Evidence for this comes from the increase in search interest for the term insomnia and related term like "insomnia remedies" and "cure for insomnia".

When more people experience insomnia, more people have also taken to meditation. The correlation coefficient between insomnia and meditation is 0.86 suggesting a strong correlation.
